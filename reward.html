Motivation and Reward

Motivation and survival are intrinsicly connected.
The fear of constant threat has pressured the human brain to evolve.
With our complex circuitry, we select the best behavior to satisfy our clashing desires.


Motivation - Featuring:


  • Hypothalamus - Maintains energy and water balance, heat regulation, circadian rhythms, defensive behaviors, and more!
  • Amygdala - Responds to outside threats, social situations, and emotional attachment.
  • Amongst the other brain structures that all work in harmony to create a decisive physical and psychological response, these two are the most prominent.

Rewards:

Thanks to the dopamine system, we are continuously stuck in a reward loop.
By definition, a reward is "any stimulus that moves the homeostatic or allostatic balance of the organism closer to its ideal set points".

There are two types of rewards:

  • Primary Rewards address basic survival functions, such as quenching thirst, eating a meal, or getting a well-needed good night of sleep.
  • Secondary Rewards are items or concepts that hold value to the individual that is indirctly associated with some primary reward. Examples include money (which can buy food), and social status (which can help with future desires).
Humans and other animals will act different in the presence of an obtainable reward. A stimulus that increases the frequency of a desirable outcome is called a reinforcer.
In the opposite direction, we have punishments, which are aversive.
